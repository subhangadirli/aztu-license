# Comparing AzTU v2.1 to Other Licenses

*Disclaimer: This comparison is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal advice. The AzTU License v2.1 has not been formally reviewed by a lawyer and is not an OSI-approved license.*

| Feature | AzTU v2.1 | GNU AGPLv3 | GNU GPLv3 | MIT |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Philosophy** | Perpetual openness | Perpetual openness | Perpetual openness | Do whatever you want |
| **Copyleft (Derivative works must share same license)** | Yes | Yes | Yes | No |
| **Network / SaaS Copyleft (Closes the "SaaS Loophole")**| Yes | Yes | No | No |
| **Language & Readability** | Explicit, simplified | Complex, traditional legal | Complex, traditional legal | Short, simplified |
| **OSI Approved** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| **Legally Reviewed** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |

#### Why choose AzTU over AGPL?
You should use the AGPL if you require a battle-tested, legally heavily scrutinized, and OSI-approved license. 

You might prefer AzTU if you are an indie developer or small team looking for a license inspired by the AGPL's philosophy, but written in a simplified, explicit manner that focuses heavily on modern SaaS realities without the historical density of GNU licenses.
