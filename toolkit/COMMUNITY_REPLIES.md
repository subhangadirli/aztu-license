# Community Reply Templates

When you adopt a non-OSI license like AzTU, you will inevitably get GitHub Issues asking "Why didn't you just use MIT/GPL?". 

Here are copy-paste replies to keep the conversation polite, professional, and shut down toxic debates.

### Scenario 1: "Why not MIT?"
> "Thanks for the question! We chose AzTU because we want to ensure this project remains a public good. Under MIT, massive cloud providers could wrap our work into a paid SaaS without ever contributing back. AzTU ensures that if someone profits from our work as a service, they must share their improvements with the community."

### Scenario 2: "Why not AGPL?"
> "Great question. We love the AGPL's philosophy, but we found the actual legal text to be incredibly dense and confusing for everyday developers. AzTU is inspired by the AGPL but uses simplified, explicit language. We are treating it as an experimental, highly-readable alternative."

### Scenario 3: "This isn't Open Source because the OSI hasn't approved it."
> "You're legally correct—AzTU is not OSI-approved, so we are careful to refer to this as 'source-available with strong copyleft'. Our goal is perpetual openness and closing the SaaS loophole. While we respect the OSI, our primary focus right now is protecting indie development, and this license serves that goal best for us today."
