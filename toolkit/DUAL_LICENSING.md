# Dual Licensing & Monetization Guide

Because the AzTU License v2.1 strictly prevents closed-source SaaS wrapping, it creates a powerful opportunity for **Dual Licensing**. 

If a company wants to use your AzTU-licensed software in their proprietary, closed-source SaaS application without sharing their source code, they must buy a **Commercial License** from you.

### How to set up Dual Licensing:

1. **Retain Copyright:** You MUST own 100% of the copyright to your code. If you accept PRs from the community, you must use a CLA (Contributor License Agreement) or DCO (Developer Certificate of Origin) to ensure you have the right to re-license their contributions commercially.
2. **Add a Notice to your README:**
   Add this section to your repository:
   > **Commercial Licensing**
   > This software is licensed under the AzTU License v2.1, which requires derivative SaaS projects to remain open-source. If you wish to use this software in a closed-source commercial product, please contact us at [Your Email] to purchase a Commercial License.
3. **Drafting a Commercial License:** You don't need a complex legal document to start. A simple B2B software agreement granting them the right to use the software without the AzTU obligations is sufficient for early sales.
