# License Enforcement Guide (Cease & Desist Templates)

If you discover a company is using your AzTU-licensed code in their commercial SaaS without open-sourcing their derivative work, here is how you handle it.

**Step 1: Assume Good Faith**
Most license violations are accidents by junior developers who didn't read the license. 

**Step 2: The "Friendly Reminder" Email**
*Subject: Open Source License Compliance for [Your Project Name]*
> Hi [Company/Developer Name],
> 
> I'm the creator of [Your Project Name]. I noticed your team is utilizing it in [Their Product Name]. We're thrilled you find it useful!
> 
> I wanted to gently remind you that [Your Project Name] is distributed under the AzTU License v2.1. This is a strong copyleft license that requires any derivative works provided over a network (SaaS) to also be open-sourced under the same terms. 
>
> Currently, it appears [Their Product Name] is closed-source. To come into compliance, you can either:
> 1. Open-source the relevant derivative codebase under AzTU v2.1.
> 2. Purchase a commercial license from us to keep your code closed.
> 3. Remove [Your Project Name] from your systems.
>
> Let me know how you'd like to proceed! We're happy to help you transition to a commercial tier if that's easiest.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

**Step 3: Escalation**
If they ignore you after 14 days, you can issue a formal DMCA takedown notice to their hosting provider (AWS, GitHub, Vercel, etc.), citing copyright infringement due to license violation.
